Watermelon Kimchi

Put the whole food to use with this recipe

I must admit, I first got the idea to make this from a Tik Tok. As I write this, I've got a watermelon sitting on my counter, waiting to be turned into delcious kimchi. This is a rather accessible recipe that calls for a limited number of ingredients that most people will be able to find at their local grocery.

Why watermelon of all things?

I grew up eating plenty of watermelon in the muggy midwestern summers. Alas, those memories are tarnished by the myriad of watermelon rinds that we never seemed to have a use for. Watermelon rind is edible once processed and provides plenty of nutrients and a satisfying crunch similar to cabbage, so... why not try it?

Kimchi Process

Thankfully, this is not as labor intensive as making traditional kimchi!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs watermelon rind slices-peeled
  • 3 Tbsp coarse sea salt

Kimchi Seasoning

  • 6 cloves of garlic-minced
  • 5 Tbsp gochugaru/coarse red chili flakes
  • 2 Tbsp honey

Garnish

  • chopped green onions
  • sesame seeds

Optional extras

  • ⅓ cup of watermelon juice-blended
